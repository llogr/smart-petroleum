OUR TEAM

The Smart Petroleum Management Team consists of individuals with extensive experience in both project management and academia. Together we have the technical know-how and practical expertise to oversee and develop offshore drilling capabilities.

COLIN R GAMBLIN
CEO

Colin has over thirty year’s experience leading and co-ordinating multidiscipline project teams within the oil and gas industry for both upstream onshore and offshore projects. A sound knowledge of the commercial and technical challenges for the successful delivery of complex projects through to project delivery. With a background in project management and construction, he has the professional skills to organise complex projects where time constraints and cost targets are overriding project delivery considerations.

Colin worked as a Divisional Director with Mott MacDonald’s, Oil Gas and Petrochemicals Unit, he was responsible for project delivery from inception to completion, including pre-investment appraisal, cost and schedule management, the management and monitoring for the placing of contracts, purchase orders and service orders.

ZBIGNIEW (BISH) KUNCEWICZ
DIRECTOR

Zbigniew has spent the whole of his career (over 35 years) in Project Management and mainly in the Energy Sector, which commenced in 1967 with Ames Crosta Mills– a specialist Waste water treatment plant manufacturer and contractor, as a technical apprentice.

In 1999 he joined McDonald Envirocare Ltd as Development Director and General Manager to establish and commercialise specific applications of enabling advanced technologies into conventional water and wastewater treatment systems.In April 2001, Zbigniew joined Phoenix Partnerships Ltd which is a consultancy company providing project, processes, product and technology strategies, development and solutions to Energy Industry.

CHRISTINE STARR
DIRECTOR

Christine has spent the majority of her career (over 30 years) in the Energy industry, which commenced in 1974 with United Utilities laboratories as an analyst. In 1976 she joined Ames Crosta Mills – a specialist Waste water treatment plant manufacturer and contractor, as a process scientist during which time she undertook design aspects of sewage treatment plants and laboratory analytical work. In 1999 Christine joined McDonald Envirocare as Director for Process and Scientific services, to support the commercialisation of specific applications of enabling advanced technologies into conventional water and wastewater treatment systems

In April 2001 she joined Phoenix Partnerships Ltd. Which is a consultancy company providing project, processes, products and technology strategies, development and solutions to any water related industry and industries.

DR ROOHOLLAH MAHANI
DIRECTOR – RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT

Roohollah has over 35 years experience in academic life as well as in the business sector. He received his PhD in Civil Engineering from UMIST and also worked at the University of Manchester from 1981, after which he moved to City University in London in 1983. Following his academic career he went into industry and by 1986 he has been the technical director, project director and managing director of three successful companies involved in various consultancy ventures.

Although his speciality is Civil engineering, from his other businesses he gained a great deal of knowledge and experience in the Energy sector and especially in the Oil & Gas industries. He has a great experience in Oil & Gas Risk assessment and one of the projects he is now working on is the reliability analysis of Jack-ups.

In order to broaden his management experience, he attended an MBA course from the University of Hertfordshire and obtained an Executive MBA in 2004.

He has managed many big projects all over the world and has published over 30 technical papers in top journals

PROFESSOR LAURIE BOSWELL
SENIOR CONSULTANT

Professor Laurie Boswell is an expert in structural design and has over 30 years experience in both onshore and offshore projects. He was Head of the Department of Civil Engineering at City University from 1991 until 1998.

Laurie's professional activities have been and are currently varied. However, they support the belief that academic members of an engineering department must have close links with industry. In 1985, he was appointed as the Director of the City University Ocean Engineering Research Centre. He has acted as a consultant to BP, Wimpey Corporation, Noble Denton, Global Maritime, the Steel Construction Institute and British Steel (now Corus). In the latter, work has been carried out to develop the three latest products for application in Civil Engineering Construction. Currently, Professor Boswell is a member of the Board of Directors of the International Society of Offshore and Polar Engineering and a member of the Independent Advisory Board of The American Bureau of Shipping. He is a member of the Education and Research and Development Committees of the Society of Underwater Technology and sits on European Committees for Construction and Education.

Laurie has published relevant material in internationally recognised journals such as, Geotechnique, Proc. of the Institution of Civil Engineering, International Journal of Solids and Structures and the International Journal of Constructional Steelwork. Alternatively and appropriately some material has been more effectively disseminated in special publications, such as Her Majesty's Stationary Office and special editions of the International Journal of Marine Structures.

Laurie is the organiser of the international conference on “The Jack-up Platform- Design, Construction & Operation” at City University. The first conference was held in 1985 and the successive biannual conferences have been a great success. The conferences have proved to be an excellent forum for the exchange of ideas and the discussion of recent developments in the offshore oil and gas industry particularly related to jack-up technology. They have also contributed to the development of code of practice.

Professor Boswell has been responsible for writing a textbook and editing several others, including Platform Superstructures, Design and Construction and The Jack-up Drilling Platform. In all, he has published over 100 papers in journals, conferences and contributions to textbooks.
